The expansion ideas were presented to the entire team at the next formal meeting. Everyone chose the areas they would like to write up. Most, if not all meeting records were done by Michelle, Blayne, and sometimes Jake. Most citations were formatted correctly by Charlie, Helia, Jake and Blayne. On a final note, although some people did contribute in areas that were not assigned to them, these were done willingly because it has been engaging and pleasant to work alongside each other.

Across the weeks of the assignment, formal meetings were held twice a week, recorded for ~30 mins, however often continued for up to 6 hours. This time was not forced, and was used to collaborate in real time on joint portions of the assignment, as well as to provide help and feedback to other members of the group. Another 2-3 informal meetings were held through the week with the same real time collaboration, discussion and typically length.
As we worked on the assignment, there were points where almost each team member had one on one collaboration, spending a few hours working together to finish off sections or offer help towards difficult parts. These sessions came naturally and each team member was more than willing to help out in this aspect. This helped our assignment and team work tremendously and is an aspect we hope will continue into A3 as most of us got our best work done this way. As we came up with questions that were best answered by tutors, we assigned individual questions to group members, to give opportunity for this to be a shared task, rather than an individual feeling like it was an extra addition to their workload.
As a group there have been no arguments or hiccups or resentful feelings that have been brought to the table, which has been a great relief for most of us and our history with teamwork collaborations in the past. The only thing that didn’t work well in our group was a couple people not meeting deadlines due to real life happening, although no one blames them of course. They still always showed up with their work that was assigned, just a little later than what the rest would have liked. Next time, to help fix this, could be an option to discuss prior commitments and work schedules more in depth to assign more appropriate sections, especially if someone falls behind; instead of handing in the full assigned section late, they could hand in what they have done already for someone else to finish, and find another area to work on when they have more time or assign shorter, but better quality sections to those with less available time.

In the middle of the assignment period, workload was discussed and contribution percentages were predetermined, up for future change. These were relatively similar, with higher percentages reflective of workload and effort. In the final week some of these values were changed to appropriately reflect actual contributions of each team member. In doing this it was clear that some group members had performed a bit more on certain final aspects of the assignment than others, with life getting in the way which is understandable. We hope to keep this transparency in A3 as it immediately sets expectations of workload that can be reflected on towards the final deadline. In the final days, we had a hard but necessary talk where everyone anonymously submitted the % contribution they thought they had done and then we went through each answer and spoke about why it was so low or high and came to an agreement on everyone’s % contribution. The conversation definitely highlighted our fortitude as a team and we are grateful that there have been no bad experiences in any part of doing this assignment together.
