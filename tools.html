Microsoft Teams was our main discussion and meeting application. Regularly scheduled meetings and many impromptu meetings were held to discuss issues and progression and collaborate in real-time with each other. We created a group chat in Microsoft Teams to speak to each other more efficiently as the 'teams' tab functions more like Facebook statuses which is not ideal. Youtube was used to host our IT Interview.
We used Google Docs to collaborate on our assignment drafts across four documents that included the assignment, meeting notes, agendas and actions, and a general thoughts and ideas document. Blayne, Jake and Michelle added most, if not all, of the contributions to the meeting records. This includes creating agendas, making sure all drafts are formatted correctly and citing all references correctly.
Canva.com was used to polish the assignment report, utilising the ability to provide appropriate visual elements. Blayne, Helia and Michelle mainly contributed to this. Michelle also used it to create our group logo and create the icon mockups for our rewards section for the proposed application.
We used teleporthq.io as a template for our website; this enabled collaboration and the ability to see others work in real-time without waiting for Git commits. The most commitments to the website have been Jake and Michelle, with Charlie and Blayne working alongside Michelle's help to add stuff too.
We divided the contributions into three people polishing the website and three people polishing the two required reports the last couple of days before submission, however this division was not kept to due to availabilities with some group members and the other members wanting the reports and websites done ASAP. We did not use Github.com until the very last moment as we chose to make all of our contributions across other websites that enabled real-time contributions.
